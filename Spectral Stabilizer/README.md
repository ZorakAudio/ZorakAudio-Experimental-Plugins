# Spectral Stabilizer
### Tonal balance stabilizer — smooths spectral swings without obvious EQ pumping

**Spectral Stabilizer keeps a sound’s “tone shape” steadier over time.**
It’s useful when material swings between harsh and dull, or when resonances flare up and vanish.

---

## What it’s good at

- Vocals that change tone phrase-to-phrase
- Pads that get “spiky” when notes overlap
- Busses where you want consistency without static EQ

---

## Controls

### 1) Sigma
Smoothing amount/window (higher = steadier, slower changes).

### 2) Depth
How much correction is applied.

### 3) MotionBias
Follow vs hold:
- Higher follows motion more
- Lower resists changes more

---

## Quick Start

- Sigma: 1–3  
- Depth: 20–50  
- MotionBias: 40–70  

---

## Safety / behavior notes

- Very high Sigma + high Depth can make things feel “flattened.”
- Always level-match when judging.
