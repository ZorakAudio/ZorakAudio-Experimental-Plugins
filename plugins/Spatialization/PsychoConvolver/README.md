# PsychoConvolver

## What it is
PsychoConvolver is an **IR-based space processor** with perceptual macro controls layered on top of convolution.

Load an IR into **slot 0**, then shape how that IR behaves with broad psychoacoustic controls instead of only treating it as a static impulse response player.

---

## Why use it
Use it when you want the realism and complexity of convolution, but still want musical macro controls for how that space presents itself.

---

## Quick start
1. Load the convolution IR into **slot 0**.
2. Set **Wet** for overall blend.
3. Use **Body**, **Clarity**, and **Air** to rebalance how the IR feels tonally.
4. Use **Envelopment** and **Distance** to change how wrapped or far the space feels.
5. Use **Somatic** when you want more physical weight from the result.

---

## Main controls
### Wet
Overall wet blend.

### Body
Shifts the IR toward more or less body-weight.

### Clarity
Emphasizes or relaxes the articulate, forward part of the response.

### Envelopment
How wrapped-around and immersive the space feels.

### Distance
Pushes the result nearer or farther.

### Air
Upper openness and airiness of the space.

### Somatic
Perceived physical weight / bodily feel.

### Output
Final output amount.

---

## Routing / notes
This is a normal stereo in / stereo out processor.

The extra file slot is central to the workflow: no IR, no convolution.

---

## In one sentence
PsychoConvolver lets you start with a real IR and then steer the perceptual feel of that space with macro controls.
